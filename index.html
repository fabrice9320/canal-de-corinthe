THE CORINTH CANAL

How man created an island

The Corinth Canal, an engineering marvel nestled in Greece's landscape, showcases human ingenuity and bravery against nature's challenges. Carved through the Isthmus of Corinth, connecting the Gulf of Corinth to the Aegean Sea, this narrow canal has been a vital passage for global maritime trade.

Its construction, begun in 1882 and completed in 1893, was a daring endeavor, requiring decades of effort and technical progress. Stretching 6.4 kilometers in length and 25 meters in width, the Corinth Canal significantly reduced the time and distance of voyages between the Adriatic and the Aegean Sea, thus marking a milestone in the history of navigation.
